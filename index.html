🎨 Reading Background Colour

Some people find a different background colour more comfortable to read on than white — the same idea as coloured overlays or tinted exercise books some schools use. Try one and see what feels best for you. There's no wrong answer, and you can change back any time.

Cream
Blue
Green
Rose
Grey

Honest note: research on coloured backgrounds and reading is actually mixed — they're not a proven fix for dyslexia specifically, but they're completely harmless and some people genuinely find them more comfortable. Worth trying if white feels too bright or "busy" to read.

📖 Why we built this

A lot of SATs revision costs money — some sites charge £20 a month. That's not fair on families who can't afford it, so everything here is free, for good.

Kids learn differently. Some are autistic, some have ADHD, some are dyslexic, some are just starting out, some are ready to be pushed further — so there are four difficulty levels, no timers anywhere, gentle break reminders, and a reading colour option for anyone who finds white pages hard to read.

None of it is a cure or a trick. It's just trying to make sure a child never looks at a page and thinks "I can't do this" before they've even started.
